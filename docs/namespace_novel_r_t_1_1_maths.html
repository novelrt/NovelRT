Calculates the product of the provided GeoVector3F and the provided scalar.

An example of a multiplication between a three-dimensional vector and a scalar:

\begin{align*} 4 \times \begin{pmatrix} 8\\ 6\\ 3 \end{pmatrix} &\rightarrow \begin{pmatrix} 4\\ 4\\ 4 \end{pmatrix} \circ \begin{pmatrix} 8\\ 6\\ 3 \end{pmatrix}\\ &= \begin{pmatrix} 4 \times 8 \\ 4 \times 6 \\ 4 \times 3 \end{pmatrix}\\ &= \begin{pmatrix} 32\\ 24\\ 12 \end{pmatrix} \end{align*}

This is a pure method. Calling this without using the result has no effect and can introduce overhead for calling the method.

Parameters
lhsThe scalar that gets multiplied by the GeoVector3F's components.
rhsThe GeoVector3F whose components get multiplied by the scalar.
Returns
A GeoVector3F where the components consist of the product of this and the provided scalar.
